Explore Sarawak

Popular Destinations

Kuching - The Cat City

Discover Sarawak's charming capital with its colonial architecture, vibrant markets, and the iconic Kuching Waterfront. Don’t miss the Sarawak Museum, showcasing the state's history and culture.

Bako National Park

Embark on an adventure in Sarawak's oldest national park, Bako. Experience diverse ecosystems, from mangrove swamps to rainforest jungles, and encounter wildlife like proboscis monkeys and bearded pigs.

Gunung Mulu National Park

Explore the UNESCO World Heritage site of Gunung Mulu National Park, famous for its limestone karst formations, vast cave systems, and the challenging Pinnacles trail. The park is a haven for spelunkers and nature enthusiasts.

Semenggoh Wildlife Centre

Get up close and personal with the endangered orangutans at the Semenggoh Wildlife Centre. Witness the rehabilitation efforts and learn about the conservation of these majestic creatures in their natural habitat.

Sarawak Cultural Village

Step into the living museum of Sarawak Cultural Village to experience the traditional lifestyles of various ethnic groups. Enjoy cultural performances, traditional crafts, and the warmth of Sarawakian hospitality.

Niah Cave

Uncover the mysteries of Niah Cave, home to one of the world's largest cave entrances and archaeological sites. Discover ancient cave paintings and explore the historical significance of this natural wonder.
